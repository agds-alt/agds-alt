# Cold Email Outreach Guide

## üéØ Why Cold Emails Work

**Stats:**
- 50% of jobs are filled before posting
- Cold emails have 5-15% response rate
- Founders/CTOs read emails from talented devs
- Less competition than public job boards

**When to use:**
- Dream companies not hiring publicly
- Startups in your tech stack
- After seeing their GitHub/tech blog
- Companies you genuinely admire

## üîç Finding Companies to Email

### **Method 1: Tech Stack Match**

**Find companies using your tech:**

1. **Search GitHub:**
   ```
   "Next.js" "tRPC" "careers"
   "built with Next.js" "we're hiring"
   ```

2. **Check Vercel Showcase:**
   - https://vercel.com/customers
   - Filter for companies using Next.js
   - Check if they're hiring

3. **Search Twitter/X:**
   ```
   "Next.js" "React" "hiring"
   "tRPC" "looking for"
   "TypeScript engineer" "remote"
   ```

4. **Check Tech Blogs:**
   - Read engineering blogs
   - If they write about your stack ‚Üí reach out

---

### **Method 2: Y Combinator Companies**

**YC companies = well-funded, fast-growing:**

1. Go to: https://www.ycombinator.com/companies
2. Filter:
   - Batch: Recent (W24, S24, W25)
   - Industry: B2B, SaaS, Developer Tools, Fintech
   - Team Size: 2-50
   - Status: Active
3. Check their tech stack (job posts, GitHub, product)
4. Reach out if using Next.js/TypeScript/Python

**Why YC companies:**
- Have funding (can pay well)
- Growing fast (always hiring)
- Want talent quickly
- Open to cold outreach

## üîç How to Find Email Addresses

### **Method 1: LinkedIn**
- Many profiles list email
- Connect first, then message
- Ask politely for email

### **Method 2: Company Website**
- Check "About" or "Team" page
- Common formats:
  - firstname@company.com
  - firstname.lastname@company.com
  - f.lastname@company.com

### **Method 3: Email Finders (Tools)**

**Free:**
- Hunter.io (50 free searches/month)
- RocketReach (5 free/month)
- Voila Norbert (50 free/month)

**How to use:**
1. Enter company domain
2. Enter person's name
3. Get email + verification

### **Method 4: GitHub**
- Check company's GitHub org
- Find commits from team
- Many devs list email in commits

```bash
git log --format='%an <%ae>' | sort -u
```

### **Method 5: Twitter/X Bio**
- Many founders list email in bio
- Or DM: "Love your product. What's best email to reach you?"

## üö® What NOT to Do

**Avoid:**
- ‚ùå Generic "To whom it may concern"
- ‚ùå Long emails (> 200 words)
- ‚ùå Begging for job
- ‚ùå Spelling mistakes in company name
- ‚ùå Mass BCCing (they can tell)
- ‚ùå Attachments in first email (resume can come later)
- ‚ùå Following up daily (1 week minimum)
- ‚ùå Being rude if rejected

---

Before hitting send:

- [ ] Short (< 150 words)
- [ ] GitHub link included
- [ ] No typos
- [ ] Clear value proposition
- [ ] Easy to reply (ask simple question)
- [ ] Professional email signature
- [ ] Correct recipient name spelling

## üé¨ Sample Complete Workflow

### **Week 1-2: Research Phase**
1. Find 50 target companies
2. Research each (tech stack, funding, team size)
3. Find email addresses for CTOs/founders
4. Prioritize top 30

### **Week 3-4: Outreach Phase**
1. Send 5-10 emails/day
2. Personalize each one
3. Track in spreadsheet
4. Respond to replies within 24 hours

### **Week 5-6: Follow-up Phase**
1. Follow up on no-response after 5-7 days
2. Continue sending to new companies
3. Book calls with interested companies
4. Ask for referrals from rejections

### **Week 7-8: Results Phase**
1. Complete interviews
2. Receive offers
3. Negotiate
4. Choose best fit

## üí° Pro Tips

1. **Quality > Quantity** - 10 personalized emails > 50 generic
2. **GitHub is everything** - Make sure pinned repos are impressive
3. **Be persistent, not annoying** - 2 follow-ups max
4. **Time it right** - Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM their time
5. **Research first** - Know their product/tech before emailing
6. **Show, don't tell** - GitHub link > long descriptions
7. **Keep it short** - Busy people skim emails
8. **Ask questions** - "Are you hiring?" is easier to answer than long pitch
9. **Be genuine** - Only reach out if you actually like the company
10. **Learn from rejections** - Ask for feedback
